Shenandoah National Park is a dream for dog owners. Unlike most national parks that severely restrict pets, Shenandoah welcomes dogs on nearly every trail — over 480 miles of hiking with your best friend.

## Dog Rules at a Glance

- Dogs allowed on all but ~20 trails (mostly short nature walks)
- 6-foot leash required at all times — no exceptions
- Dogs welcome in campgrounds and picnic areas
- Not allowed in park buildings or on some ranger-led programs
- Pack out all waste

## Best Dog-Friendly Trails

### Old Rag Mountain (9 miles, strenuous)
The park's most popular hike. The rock scramble section requires you to lift your dog over boulders — only attempt with an athletic, confident dog under 60 lbs. The reward is one of the best views on the East Coast.

### Whiteoak Canyon (9.4 miles, moderate)
Six waterfalls along the trail. Your dog will love the creek crossings. Trail can be muddy in spring — bring a towel for the car.

### Dark Hollow Falls (1.4 miles, easy)
Shortest hike to a waterfall in the park. Perfect for puppies building trail fitness or older dogs who can't do long distances anymore.

### Stony Man Trail (1.6 miles, easy)
Paved and wide. Incredible panoramic views from the second-highest peak in the park. Dog-friendly and wheelchair-accessible.

## Best Time to Visit

**Fall (October):** Peak foliage is spectacular but trails are packed. Go midweek if possible.

**Spring (April-May):** Wildflowers, fewer crowds, and comfortable temps. Trails can be muddy.

**Summer:** Hot and humid at lower elevations. Stick to higher-elevation trails and start early.

**Winter:** Many facilities close but trails remain open. Pack layers and traction for icy sections.

## Practical Tips

- Water is available at developed areas but bring your own for the trail — at least 1oz per pound of dog per hour of hiking
- Skyline Drive has many overlooks where you can let your dog stretch
- Cell service is unreliable — download trail maps before you go
- Tick season runs March through November — check your dog after every hike
